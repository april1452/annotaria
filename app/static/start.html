What is AnnOtaria?

AnnOtaria is a collaborative article annotation application. AO allows users to comment, cite, and even link other articles in a common environment, facilitating collaboration and discussion. AO technology focuses on OWL and RDF ontology.

Getting Started

Reading

AnnOtaria starts in Reader Mode. To change articles, click on the "Articles" button on the top navigation bar.

Annotations

In Reader Mode, you may only read existing annotations. Annotations on the entire article, such as author or publishing year, are displayed to the right. To display annotations made on specific text in the article, switch to the "Filter" tab to choose which annotations to view.

Switching to Annotator Mode

Annotations can only be made in Annotator Mode. To switch from Reader Mode to Annotator Mode, click the Mode button on the far right of the navigation bar. You will be prompted for your name and email, both of which are required. To switch back to Reader Mode, click the Mode button again.

Creating and Editing Annotations

To create annotations, click the "Add" button on the right of the navigation bar. To make an annotation on a specific part of the article, click "Add" after selecting the text you want to annotate. To edit your unsaved annotations, click on the "Edit" tab (next to the "Filter" tab). Here you can edit and delete your annotations.

Saving Annotations

To save your annotations, click the "Save" button on the right of the navigation bar. Remember, saving is permanent! Once you save your annotations, you cannot edit or delete them. However, if you don't save your annotations before switching articles or leaving Annotator Mode, your annotations will be lost.
